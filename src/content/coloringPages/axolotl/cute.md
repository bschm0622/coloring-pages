Real axolotls are typically pink or pale, but the coloring page format means anything goes. Many kids go straight for bright pink or purple — which is honestly perfect. The gill plumes on top of the head are the most fun part to color, and the big friendly eyes are easy to fill in even for younger kids.

This design focuses on the face and upper body, which is where all the interesting detail is on an axolotl. Simple enough for ages 4 and up.
